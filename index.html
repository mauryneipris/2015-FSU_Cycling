About Us

Our purpose as the Cycling Club at Florida State University is to encourage all people to participate in cycling.

Cycling is a fun activity that can be recreation, transportation, fitness and competition. We work to organize, promote and support cycling events for our members and community. We strive to establish members as community supporters, loyal activists and cycling sport leaders.

The club is open to all cyclists of every discipline and ability, from new riders to pros. We focus on developing riders into better and safer cyclists with club rides almost everyday. We compete collegiately in the South East Collegiate Cycling Conference (SECCC), as well as other cycling events.


Join The Club

General Information
You are welcome to join us on a few rides before we ask for all your paperwork and club dues.
-We suggest riding with us a few times before you make up your mind on cycling. Cycling can be intimidating on your first time
(out of shape, not used to traffic, etc), give it a good try and you will see quick improvements!
Requirements
-To join FSU Cycling, you must be a student, staff or faculty at Florida State University.
-To race collegiately, you must be a full time student at Florida State University
-And finally, you need to pay yearly club dues. (Helps us operate the club)
-No cycling experience is needed to join, we can teach you!
What You Need
-A bicycle
-A helmet
-Dedication to ride!
Membership
Membership is divided into 2 different categories:

-Club Membership($20): is designed for club members who prefer to ride their bikes for recreation, commuting, socializing and having
fun! There is no pressure to race or train, and club rides are a lot more relaxed.

-Race Membership($50): is designed for members who plan on racing for the team in the South East Collegiate Cycling Conference.
The race team rides are taken a bit more seriously to prepare for upcoming races.
How To Join The Club
1. Visit FSU Cycling Club's page on FSU Campus Recreation's website.
2. Fill out the Sport Club Waiver. If you need help, check out FSU Campus Rec's guide here.
